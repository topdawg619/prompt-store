## The #1 Mistake Content Creators Make with ChatGPT

Treating ChatGPT like a search engine.

"Write me a YouTube script about productivity" produces a generic, boring script. You'd never post it.

The solution is **context-rich prompting**: giving ChatGPT your voice, your audience, your angle, and your format before asking for anything.

Here's the shift:

❌ "Write a YouTube script about productivity."  
✅ "Write a 7-minute YouTube script for my channel [channel name], which targets burned-out 9-to-5 workers in their late 20s who want to start a side hustle. My tone is direct, slightly sarcastic, and motivational — like a friend who's already done what they're trying to do. The topic is 'Why your morning routine is killing your productivity.' Open with a hook that challenges conventional wisdom. Use short paragraphs. Include a CTA at the 5-minute mark for my free newsletter."

That's the difference between unusable and ready-to-record.

## YouTube Script Prompts

**Script hook formula:**
> "Write 5 YouTube video hook options for the topic '[topic]'. Each hook should be under 30 seconds when spoken aloud, create immediate curiosity or controversy, and speak directly to [target audience]. Avoid starting with 'Have you ever...'"

**Full script formula:**
> "Write a [length]-minute YouTube script for [channel name] on the topic '[topic]'. My audience is [description]. Tone: [tone]. Structure: hook → problem agitation → solution → proof/example → CTA. Keep sentences short. Flag where B-roll would help with [B-ROLL] notes."

---

## Newsletter Prompts

Your newsletter is your most valuable owned channel. These prompts help you write it faster without losing your voice.

**Subject line generator:**
> "Write 10 email subject lines for a newsletter about [topic]. Mix styles: curiosity gaps, bold claims, personal stories, numbered lists. Target open rate: high. Audience: [audience description]. Avoid clickbait that doesn't deliver."

**Newsletter body formula:**
> "Write a 400-word newsletter in the style of a personal essay for [newsletter name]. Topic: [topic]. My voice is [adjectives: e.g., warm, nerdy, brutally honest]. Include one actionable tip the reader can use today. End with a question to drive replies. First person, conversational. No corporate language."

---

## Twitter/X Thread Prompts

Threads that go viral aren't accidents. They're structured.

**Thread formula:**
> "Write a Twitter thread about [topic] for an audience of [audience]. Start with a bold, counter-intuitive claim. Follow with 7–10 tweets that each deliver one clear insight. Each tweet under 250 characters. End with a tweet that teases more value and links to [destination]. No filler. Every tweet must stand alone."

**Engagement prompt:**
> "Write 5 standalone tweets about [topic] designed to start conversations. Each should state an opinion that's slightly controversial but defensible. Under 180 characters each."

---

## Short-Form Video Scripts (TikTok / Reels / Shorts)

**Hook-first formula:**
> "Write a 60-second TikTok script for a [niche] creator on the topic '[topic]'. Open with the single most interesting sentence about this topic. Format: hook (5 seconds) → 3 key points (45 seconds) → CTA (10 seconds). Conversational tone. No jargon. End with a question to drive comments."

**Series formula:**
> "Create a 5-part TikTok series outline about [topic] for [audience]. Each video should be standalone but build on the previous one. Include hook ideas and main talking points for each. Make each video end in a way that makes the viewer want to see the next one."

## The Repurposing System That Saves Hours Every Week

Here's a power move: create once, repurpose everywhere.

**Master repurposing prompt:**
> "I have this [blog post / YouTube transcript / podcast episode]. Repurpose it into: (1) a Twitter thread, (2) a LinkedIn post, (3) an email newsletter, (4) 3 Instagram captions, and (5) a 60-second video script. Maintain my voice: [tone description]. Don't just summarize — find the most interesting angle for each platform."

One piece of content → 5 assets. That's a week of posting from a single source.

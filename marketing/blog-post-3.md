## 1. Customer Email Prompts

**The situation:** You get the same 15 types of customer emails on repeat. Writing responses from scratch is a productivity killer.

**Template-building prompt:**
> "You are a customer service specialist for [business name], a [type of business] that sells [products/services] to [target customer]. Write a polished, friendly email response to this customer message: '[paste message]'. Tone: [warm and professional / casual / formal]. Keep it under 150 words. End with an offer to help further. Sign off as [your name], [title]."

**FAQ batch-creation prompt:**
> "Based on these 10 common customer questions about my [product/service] business, write 10 FAQ answers. Keep each under 100 words. Tone: clear, friendly, confident. Questions: [paste list]."

**Why this matters:** Templating your 15 most common responses means customer emails go from 30 minutes to 5 minutes a day.

## 2. Marketing Copy Prompts

**Ad copy (Facebook / Instagram / Google):**
> "Write 3 Facebook ad variations for [product/service]. Target audience: [describe]. Pain point: [problem it solves]. Desired outcome: [what customer wants]. Each ad: headline (under 40 characters), primary text (under 125 characters), description (under 30 characters). CTA: [Shop Now / Learn More / Get Started]. Tone: [urgent / friendly / aspirational]."

**Product description:**
> "Write a compelling product description for [product name], a [type of product] for [target customer]. Key benefits: [list 3–5]. Tone: [conversational / premium / playful]. Format: 1 punchy headline + 3 bullet benefits + 1 closing sentence. Under 150 words total. Focus on outcomes, not features."

**Promo email:**
> "Write a promotional email for a [% off / free shipping / limited-time] sale at [business name]. Subject line options: give me 5. Email body: 200 words max. Hook with the offer, add urgency, include a clear CTA button text. Tone: [excited / exclusive / friendly]."

---

## 3. Operations & SOP Prompts

Most small business owners never document their processes — which means every hire starts from zero.

**SOP creation prompt:**
> "Create a Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) for [task/process] at a [type of business]. Steps should be numbered, clear enough for a new hire to follow without supervision, and include any decision points or exceptions. Format: Title → Purpose → Scope → Step-by-step → Notes. Keep it practical, not bureaucratic."

**Delegation brief prompt:**
> "Write a delegation brief for a virtual assistant handling [task] for my [type of business]. Include: what success looks like, the tools they'll use, communication cadence, and any common mistakes to avoid. Tone: friendly manager, not formal HR."

---

## 4. Strategy & Decision-Making Prompts

**SWOT analysis:**
> "Act as a business strategist. Conduct a SWOT analysis for my [type of business] in the [industry] market. Business details: [brief description]. Focus on practical, actionable insights — not textbook definitions. Format as a table, then summarize the top 3 strategic priorities."

**Competitor analysis:**
> "I'm launching [product/service] in the [niche] market. My main competitors are [list 3]. For each competitor, analyze: (1) their main selling point, (2) their apparent target customer, (3) a gap or weakness I could exploit. Then suggest 3 positioning angles that differentiate me."

**Pricing strategy:**
> "Help me price my [product/service]. Cost to deliver: $[X]. Target margin: [%]. Competitors charge: $[range]. My value differentiator: [describe]. Suggest 3 pricing options (economy / mid / premium) with rationale for each. Also suggest any bundling or upsell opportunities."

---

## 5. Social Media & Content Prompts

**Monthly content calendar:**
> "Create a 4-week social media content calendar for [business name] on [platform(s)]. Business: [description]. Audience: [target customer]. Post frequency: [X per week]. Content mix: [educational, promotional, behind-the-scenes, engagement — specify %]. Include post topic, format (image, video, carousel), and caption starter for each post."

**Testimonial request email:**
> "Write a friendly email asking a happy customer for a Google / [platform] review. Business: [name]. Customer's purchase: [product/service]. Tone: genuine and appreciative, not pushy. Include a direct link placeholder [REVIEW LINK]. Keep it under 100 words."
